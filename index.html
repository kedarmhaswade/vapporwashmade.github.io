My Programming Journey
When I was in 4th grade (9 years old), I was first introduced to programming by my father. I began learning JavaScript, coding small visual games such as hangman and tic-tac-toe. I eventually went on to making Node.js programs and learning a bit of Python. I loved the idea of recursion, and I created many recursive programs.
In 9th grade, I took AP CS, and so I had to learn Java (I knew the concepts but needed to learn Java syntax).
In 10th grade, I went deeper into recursion using the book "Thinking Recursively" by Eric Roberts. I got to explore a long-held interest of mine and I understood various aspects of it I had overlooked or just not learned.
In 11th grade, I discovered competitive programming, and decided to go further into it. I began learning computer science theory.
In 12th grade, I decided to go further into computer science by taking the courses: MIT's "Mathematics for CS", MIT's "Structure and Interpretation of Computer Programs", and a course on Competitive Programming.

I have learned Java, JavaScript, and Python. I have also learned a little bit of web development, along with some JS frameworks (React, Angular). I am currently learning computer science theory from two courses, Competitive Programming and Structure and Interpretation of Computer Programs (SICP).
